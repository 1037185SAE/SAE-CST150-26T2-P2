About

- Introduction to this website's creator

My name is Joel Hills, and what you're currently looking at is a website I needed to make for a Uni project. That's right. It's entirely involuntary.

Since I needed to make this website, I thought I might as well go all out. There's not much better than sex, but getting high grades is definitely one of those things.

At the time of writing, I am currently 18 years old. Young, (not really) energetic, and only just finding out the horrors of the modern adult life. So many responsibilities! So many things I need to keep track of! So many people who expect so much of me!

Before I start ranting, I'd like to give a quick introduction about me:

I'm a young adult who is interested by the inner workings of everything around me. From the largest dams and skyscrapers, to the smallest CPUs in your TV remotes. I long to know how everything works, down to the fundamental level, and admittedly, sometimes at the detriment of my own sanity.

This longing to know how things work is often paired with a critical eye looking for ways to improve said thing I'm looking at. If it can be improved, and I have the capacity and/or resources to do it, I will.

Unfortunately I often don't have the capacity to do it, and only realize partway through. This has left me with several unfinished, unpolished projects that from the outside seem like nothing but a waste of time.

I do however, learn from these unfinished projects, and I can take that knowledge into the next unfinished project, and slowly learn from there.

It's an awful way to learn, especially in a workplace where you're expected (and paid) to actually finish projects.

Hence why I've been trying to make an effort to actually finish projects, so I can get that drive to see something through to the end, and deliver on my promises.

Video Game Recommendations

- Video Games I enjoy and I think you'll enjoy too
Hollow Knight: Silksong

It took 7 years to make - and it shows. It's HARD but very rewarding. Also it's made by Australians, so if complicit nationalism is your jam, then give this a try.

Image Attribution
Stray

A game by cat lovers, for cat lovers. Almost got cancelled because the cats weren't cooperating, but they eventually got them to behave. If you like cats and cyberpunk, give this a go.

Image Attribution
Stardew Valley

A cozy farming simulator made with the sole purpose of making you envy the farmers in the countryside. Some complain that it can get quite chore-heavy, but that's because it's a simulator you fucking dumbass. It takes creative liberties where it matters to give you a more enjoyable experience.

Image Attribution
DELTARUNE

"Oh DeAr It'S pIxEl ArT tHeReFoRe TeRrIbLe!" You better cut that attitude or I'll rip off your balls. This game is far more than what it's art suggests, and has a compelling story that spans it and its predecessor, Undertale. Also the music is top-tier. Even if you don't play the game, you'll definitely enjoy the soundtrack.

Image Attribution
Celeste

A platformer game that gained its fame for being highly tuned, and having basically no skill ceiling. The sky isn't the limit for the Celeste fanbase. It combines a low-poly, pixel art, and sketchy style that works incredibly well for what it's trying to achieve. Also the protagonist is trans, so that's always a plus.

Image Attribution
Terraria

A 2D sandbox with perhaps the highest "content per dollar" ratio. There is SO much content in this game, yet its price hasn't gone up over the years. With 33 bosses, and over 5,000 items, there's something for everyone here, if those someones like 2D sandbox games. Which isn't everyone. Where am I going with this?

Image Attribution
BeamNG.Drive

A world-class 3D softbody physics engine that just so happens to also be a video game. It can realistically simulate the complex effects of not paying attention while cruising on the M1 and smashing your favourite AU Falcon.

Image Attribution
Baldur's Gate 3

The only AAA game in the last 15 years made by a studio who actually gives a fuck. This game is the perfect mixture of fun mechanics, beautiful graphics, quick wit, and a sprinkling of fleeting horniness. This game is also massive, and well worth the upfront cost, especially after mod support upped its replayability tenfold.

Image Attribution
Slay the spire 2

Basically a board game as a video game, although the video game part came first. This game pioneered the "deck building rougelite" genre that is still popular to this day. It's also one of those games that doesn't require a beefy computer to play, and is even playable at framerates that other games stop being playable at, as it doesn't require quick reflexes, just your brains.

Q&A

- Questions and answers

  • Q. Where do you see yourself in 10 years?

    • A. I'm not entirely sure. I love trains (because I'm autistic, go figure), so if I can't get a technical position, I could see myself becoming a train driver, with the hope of one day being able to drive one of those fuckass massive trains in the outback.

  • Q. What do you see as valuable assets in terms of someone's personality?

    • A. Firstly, the most valuable thing in my eyes is for someone to be an extrovert, or an introvert who knows how to pretend. Extroverts are really annoying, but they make the world go round, and so being able to know how to properly network, and how to start a friendly conversation is critical. Secondly, knowing how to deal with people's hardships is another valuable thing. If someone is sick or a loved one has passed away, and you tell them to come to work, then you need to leave. People need space, so someone who knows when it is required is very important.

  • Q. What are your most valuable assets in your personality?

    • A. Since I'm an introvert, I'm not good at socializing, and working at the back is where I thrive. Although it isn't the most developed part of my personality, I think my ability to work in a team is most valuable. If I'm not going to be working at the front and interacting with people, I need to be able to work well at the back, and working in a team is a good skill to have, as I can, with my workmates, slowly build up the company from the inside.

  • Q. What's your favourite Programming Language?

    • A. I see that as a fundamentally silly question (even though I made it), trying to get an answer regarding a topic that is fundamentally moot. Each programming language has benefits and drawbacks. No single language is perfect. It might fix issues with another language, while introducing some of its own. It's less about choosing your favourite one, and more about working with one that you feel comfortable with, and that works well for the environment its in, as well as working with the benefits to achieve a desirable outcome, while mitigating the drawbacks. People also adding social consequences for choosing the "wrong" language makes me furious. Someone shouldn't loose friends because they used Rust or React. If they feel comfortable with using it, and it works well for what's its being used for, then they should be left alone. Granted, most of these issues stem from Twitter. I think I see a good solution to this problem...
